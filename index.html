About Hiraya

"The best way to not feel hopeless is to get up and do something." – Barack Obama

Hiraya is a Filipino word meaning the fruit of one's hopes, dreams, and aspirations.

Hiraya Trust was founded by Lucy Naserian Nicholson, a Kenyan-born homeless advocate and activist who now resides in the UK. Lucy's journey began in her small hometown of Narok, Kenya, where she witnessed children and youth struggling on the streets due to poverty, broken families, domestic abuse, neglect, and policy failures.

Through volunteering and eventually starting her own organization, Lucy helped more than 30 street children and youths return to school. She pursued a Master's in Public Policy in the UK to understand how effective policies can prevent homelessness and transform lives at scale.

Our Founder's Vision

Lucy's vision is clear: to ensure that child and youth homelessness becomes a thing of the past, because children and youth are the hope and future of tomorrow.

She believes that with the right policies, holistic interventions, and advocacy, every child and youth can have hope for a brighter future in Kenya and beyond.

Our Mission

To end child and youth homelessness in Kenya and beyond by advocating for policies that address the root causes of homelessness, and by providing safe homes, education, mental health support, skills training, rehabilitation, and reintegration.

Our Vision

A world where child and youth homelessness is a thing of the past.

Our Programs

Comprehensive support designed to address every aspect of child and youth homelessness

Policy Advocacy

Working with governments and stakeholders to create and improve policies that prevent homelessness and protect vulnerable children and youth.

Shelter Provision

Providing temporary safe, dignified, and trauma-informed homes in each county for children and youth rescued from the streets.

Rehabilitation Support

Offering specialized services including counseling, substance abuse treatment, and holistic care to help children and youth heal and rebuild their lives.

Mental Health Support

Providing accessible and compassionate care that addresses trauma, promotes emotional wellbeing, and supports mental health recovery.

Education & Life Skills

Supporting children and youth in returning to school while building essential life skills for independence and personal growth.

Vocational Training

Creating opportunities for economic empowerment through practical skills training and job readiness programs tailored to local markets.

Reintegration & Long-Term Stability

We help children and youth successfully re-enter society by connecting them with permanent homes, employment, and supportive community networks for a stable future.
